French Onion Soup

Servings: 4

Prep Time: ~1h 30m

Calories per serving: ~250-450 (depending on cheese and bread)

Ingredients

  • 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp flour (optional, for thickening)
  • ½ cup dry white wine (or extra broth)
  • 4 cups beef broth (or a mix of beef and chicken)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme (or ½ tsp dried)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices French baguette
  • 1 cup grated Gruyère cheese (or mix with mozzarella and Parmesan)

Instructions

1.- Caramelize the onions:

In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions and sugar. Cook slowly, stirring often, for 35–40 minutes until deep golden brown.

2.- Build the flavor:

Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Stir in flour (if using) and cook 2 minutes. Deglaze with wine, scraping up the browned bits.

3.- Simmer the soup:

Add broth, bay leaf, thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes. Remove herbs.

4.- Toast the bread:

While the soup simmers, toast baguette slices until golden. Optionally, rub with garlic.

5.- Assemble and broil:

Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls. Top each with a toast slice and a generous heap of cheese. Broil until bubbly and golden.

6.- Serve hot:

Let cool slightly before diving in—molten cheese is no joke!
